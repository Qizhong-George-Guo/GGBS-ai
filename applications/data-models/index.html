Data, Models & AI

The Smart dimension of GGBS integrates monitoring networks, hydrologic and hydraulic models, forecasting systems, digital twins, and emerging AI tools to support planning, design, and operation of resilient water and urban systems.

The Smart Dimension of GGBS

Data and modeling tools allow planners and engineers to understand complex interactions across green, grey, and blue systems. They support decisions at every stage:

  • Assessment – mapping hazards, flows, and loads;
  • Design – testing scenarios and sizing systems;
  • Operations – managing real-time conditions;
  • Evaluation – tracking performance over time.

As climate and land use evolve, Smart tools enable adaptive and evidence-based management.

Monitoring Networks

Reliable data is the foundation of Smart infrastructure. Key elements include:

  • Rainfall and weather observations;
  • Streamflow, water-level, and groundwater gauges;
  • Tide and surge stations along coasts and estuaries;
  • Water-quality sensors (temperature, turbidity, nutrients, DO);
  • Remote-sensing imagery and LiDAR for land cover and elevation;
  • Sensor networks in green and grey infrastructure systems.

These data support model calibration, validation, and real-time decision-making.

Hydrologic, Hydraulic & Coastal Models

Models are essential tools for understanding and predicting system behavior. Common types include:

  • Hydrologic models for rainfall–runoff, soil moisture, and evapotranspiration;
  • 1D/2D hydraulic models for channel flows, floodplain inundation, and urban drainage;
  • Estuarine–coastal models for tides, salinity, waves, and storm surge;
  • Water-quality and sediment models for nutrients, turbidity, and sediment transport.

These models help test infrastructure portfolios, evaluate operations, and explore “what-if” scenarios.

Forecasting, Early Warning & Real-Time Control

Smart systems extend beyond prediction to active management:

  • Short-term forecasts of rainfall, streamflow, waves, and surge;
  • Early warning for pluvial, fluvial, and coastal flooding;
  • Real-time control of gates, pumps, and storage facilities;
  • Seasonal and long-term scenario analysis;
  • Integrated dashboards for operators and emergency managers.

These capabilities reduce risk and improve system reliability during extreme events.

AI, Machine Learning & Digital Twins

Machine learning for prediction ML models can forecast rainfall, flows, water quality, and coastal hazards using large datasets and high-frequency monitoring.
AI-driven optimization Optimization algorithms can identify efficient portfolios of green, grey, and blue measures under complex constraints.
Digital twins Coupling real-time data with models, digital twins allow operators to test scenarios, monitor performance, and make informed adjustments.
Natural language AI and decision support AI systems can assist in synthesizing documents, generating reports, summarizing monitoring data, and supporting planning workflows.

Applications Across the GGBS Dimensions

Data, models, and AI support all other GGBS application areas:

  • Optimizing green infrastructure siting and performance;
  • Evaluating grey system upgrades and operations;
  • Understanding blue system dynamics in rivers, estuaries, and coasts;
  • Monitoring, predicting, and managing flood risks;
  • Guiding Sponge City / LID district planning;
  • Tracking long-term system change under climate and land-use shifts.

Data Integration & Interoperability

Modern Smart systems rely on integrated data flows:

  • GIS layers for land use, hydrology, infrastructure, and hazards;
  • APIs for sensor networks, forecasts, and modeling platforms;
  • Cloud-based storage and analysis pipelines;
  • Visualization dashboards and web maps;
  • Cross-agency data sharing and governance structures.

These elements help ensure that planning, engineering, and operations are aligned and efficient.

Connections to Other GGBS Applications

The Smart dimension underpins all other GGBS applications:

  • Urban Stormwater – GI performance, drainage models, monitoring.
  • Watersheds – hydrology, sediment, water quality, reservoir operations.
  • Estuaries & Tidal Rivers – tides, salinity, water quality, sensors.
  • Coasts & Shorelines – wave, surge, and beach models.
  • Flood Risk & Resilience – forecasting and early warning.
  • Sponge City – planning and optimization tools.
