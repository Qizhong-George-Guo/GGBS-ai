Applications

Applications of the GGBS Framework

The GGBS Framework can be applied across urban catchments, watersheds, estuaries, and coastal systems. This section highlights how Green, Grey, Blue, and Smart elements are combined to improve resilience, water quality, and ecosystem health in real-world contexts.

Application Areas

Urban Stormwater

Green–grey retrofits, drainage networks, GI performance, and flood mitigation.

Watershed Management

Floodplain systems, reservoirs, land-use impacts, and water-quality strategies.

Estuaries & Tidal Rivers

Salinity, tidal dynamics, sediment, and nature-based estuary restoration.

Coasts & Shorelines

Beaches, dunes, coastal storms, surge, and hybrid protection solutions.

Flood Risk & Resilience

Integrated approaches for pluvial, fluvial, coastal, and compound flooding.

Sponge City / Low-Impact

Distributed GI, permeable surfaces, and urban cooling strategies.

Data, Models & AI

Monitoring networks, forecasting, digital twins, and decision support.

Why Applications Matter

While the Framework describes the four dimensions individually, the Applications show how they interact. Real systems seldom align with a single dimension. Urban districts, watersheds, estuaries, and coasts require solutions that cross boundaries:

  • Green upstream measures reduce the load on grey drainage systems.
  • Grey conveyance connects urban runoff to blue rivers and estuaries.
  • Blue systems shape flood behavior and water quality.
  • Smart systems monitor, model, and optimize them all.

Each application area in this section demonstrates the GGBS principle: co-design, co-benefits, and system-scale thinking.
